Joe's Story

Joe Reiner taught Spanish levels 1-5 for 8 years in the Twin Cities in Minnesota. He has utilized dozens of tech tools, apps, programs, and devices in his classroom over the years, often being a part of implementation process. Joe always trys to understand his student's concerns and make solutions for them, as they can be a complex "user". Teaching online courses in 2020-2023 got Joe interested in educational technology and the design process.

Joe became interested in the UX field while seeing the many parallels between being an educator and a designer who advocates for the user. He completed a UX/UI/FE Design certification through the University of Minnesota. Joe's favorite part about the design process is identifying specific user challenges and making solutions. He also enjoys information architecture, as this was a major part of his online course creation. Setting up a seamless digital curriculum and learning environment has always been important to Joe. Plus, being animated and detailed, you can always get a good story from him.

Joe explains, "I've had a diverse experience in the world of education and I am eager to offer some of my perspective towards design. I love creating a story, making prototypes, and seeing the process come to life." Joe can speak Spanish and is code-conversational with HTML, CSS, and some JavaScript. His favorite design tool is Figma, where he enjoys the prototyping process. He is currently practicing his coding skills through a variety of sources and trainings, along with learning more about analytics through Google's Analytics Academy. Check out one of his case studies below to get an idea of his design style. The case study Class Track is his favorite, as it helps solve a continuous problem he faced in all public schools.
